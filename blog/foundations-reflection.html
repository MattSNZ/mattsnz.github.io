Reflections on Foundations

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?

The first thing I've learned is that I need to trick myself into learning things that seem unappealing to me at first, however once I'm started I'll happily dive into all manner of rabbitholes within the topic at hand.

The second thing, which isn't just me but amazed me anyway, was that simply knowing about neuroplasticity has a benefit on learning. It makes sense in a way, I guess. Knowing that your brain is capable of learning all manner of new things is bound to help you learn them.

Finally, I've learned that I really need to stay on top of my focus. If I let it drift from the intention of what I'm working, it can be gone for a long time. Setting myself up for success here is key! Closing or hiding distracting browser windows and/or Slack/disabling notifications etc are key.

What are the role of values, empathy and self awareness in learning and programming?

We've seen over the last few years what can happen when people write code without values. Just because we can, definitely doesn't necessarily mean that we should. Empathy and self-awareness are very important for building websites and/or applications that are usable by many people and not just the most technologically literate.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Other than the benefits of learning about neuroplasticity, the thing that has surprised me the most has been the benefits of pseudocode. Now I just need to remind myself to use it more frequently!

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

At times, the core learning has felt like a distraction from the tech learning. Reminding myself that I'm not hear to just learn the technology but actually how to be a great developer helps me move through that.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Development is a constantly shifting field. What was common 10, or even 5, years ago is now pass√© so learning how to implement the new shiny framework or updated standards is incredibly important.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practising programming instead? Justify your answer.

I don't think so. Some of it was rehashing previously learned things for me, but it was good to go over them again in the context of development. I think my previous answer about the shifting sands of web development justifies this feeling quite well.
